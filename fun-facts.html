1. The Sun is Actually White, Not Yellow!

From space, the Sun appears bright white. Its "yellow" hue on Earth is due to our atmosphere scattering shorter wavelengths of light.

2. A Day on Venus is Longer Than Its Year!

Venus takes 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis but only 225 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun.

3. Neutron Stars Are Incredibly Dense

A single teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh around 6 billion tons. That's about the same as Mount Everest!
